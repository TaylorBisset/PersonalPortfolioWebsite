About Me

I'm a Computer Science student at Western Washington University, having graduated with honors from Everett Community College. With a deep passion for game development, software engineering, and web design, I strive to merge technical expertise with creative expression, crafting innovative and functional solutions.

My journey into technology has been shaped by diverse experiences, including military service, leadership roles, and program management. These experiences have honed my ability to think critically, solve complex problems, and lead teams effectively. I thrive in fast-paced environments that require adaptability and strategic decision-making, skills that directly translate into my work in software development.

I am constantly expanding my expertise in object-oriented programming, game design, web development, and cybersecurity. My projects involve exploring new technologies, frameworks, and programming languages, reinforcing a hands-on, self-driven approach to learning. Whether it's building a financial tracking web app, developing a 2D educational game, or diving into penetration testing, I enjoy taking on challenges that push my boundaries.

Beyond technology, I am an advocate for lifelong learning and knowledge sharing. I have experience in teaching and curriculum development, particularly in areas like wilderness survival, health science, and programming. My goal is to bridge the gap between education and technology, empowering others to develop technical skills in an engaging way.

Outside of my academic and professional pursuits, I enjoy spending time with my family, exploring the outdoors, and analyzing video game mechanics.
